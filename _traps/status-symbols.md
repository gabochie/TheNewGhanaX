## Description {#description}

The relentless prioritization of **image over impact**. Flashy displays of wealth, position, and status while the systems and institutions that actually matter crumble.

## The Effect {#the-effect}

Resources flow to appearances, not substance. Politicians drive V8s while hospitals lack beds. Influence becomes performative. Real development is starved while status is fed.

## Real-Life Symptoms {#real-life-symptoms}

- **V8 Land Cruisers** for politicians in a developing nation
- **Designer shoes** for broke influencers
- **Hospitals lacking beds** while officials build mansions
- **Big weddings, small marriages** — ceremony over relationship
- **Fake it till you make it** taken to extremes
- **Social media** full of rented lifestyles
- **Big churches, small impact** — impressive buildings, little community transformation

## Why It Persists {#why-it-persists}

- **Insecurity** — When you doubt your worth, you perform it
- **Hierarchy signaling** — Status proves you "made it" in a stratified society
- **Colonial residue** — Imported luxury as proof of civilization
- **Weak accountability** — No consequences for misplaced priorities
- **Cultural celebration** of visible wealth, regardless of source

## How to Break Free {#how-to-break-free}

1. **Measure impact, not image** — Judge by what changed, not what looked good.

2. **Invest in substance** — Put money where it matters, not where it's seen.

3. **Call out performative leadership** — Ask what was built, not what was bought.

4. **Reject false status markers** — You don't need to prove worth through display.

5. **Support substance over flash** — Promote people doing real work quietly.

6. **Define success internally** — What matters to you, not what impresses others.

## The Shift {#the-shift}

From: *"Look at what I have"*

To: *"Look at what we built"*
