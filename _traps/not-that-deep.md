## Description {#description}

The reflexive avoidance of serious issues through **jokes, sarcasm, or casual dismissal**. When confronted with real problems, the response is "it's not that deep"—minimizing what needs addressing.

## The Effect {#the-effect}

Escapism over engagement. Real issues are buried under layers of humor and apathy. Nothing gets solved because nothing is allowed to be serious. A culture of avoidance that masquerades as chill.

## Real-Life Symptoms {#real-life-symptoms}

- **Corruption scandals** met with memes instead of outrage
- **"Don't take things seriously"** as advice for every problem
- **Serious conversations** derailed with jokes
- **Activism mocked** as "being too serious"
- **Deep issues** reduced to "Ghana be village" punchlines
- **Political critique** dismissed as "you think too much"
- **Emotional suppression** — "why are you crying? It's not that deep"

## Why It Persists {#why-it-persists}

- **Coping mechanism** — Humor softens pain that's too heavy to bear straight
- **Learned helplessness** — If you can't fix it, laugh at it
- **Social pressure** — Serious people are "boring" or "difficult"
- **Fear of conflict** — Jokes deflect confrontation
- **Trauma response** — Generations of unaddressed pain

## How to Break Free {#how-to-break-free}

1. **Allow yourself to feel** — Some things *are* that deep. Let them be.

2. **Be serious sometimes** — Not everything needs a punchline.

3. **Engage without mockery** — You can critique without memes.

4. **Support serious voices** — Don't shut down people who go deep.

5. **Action over jokes** — Channel that energy into solutions.

6. **Know when humor helps vs. hides** — Laugh to cope, not to avoid.

## The Shift {#the-shift}

From: *"Relax, it's not that deep"*

To: *"Actually, it is that deep—and that's why we need to fix it"*
