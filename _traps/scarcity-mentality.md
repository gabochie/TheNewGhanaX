## Description {#description}

Scarcity mentality is the belief that **there's not enough to go around**—that someone else's gain is your loss, that wealth is a fixed pie, and that you must hoard or compete ruthlessly because resources are finite.

## The Effect {#the-effect}

Crabs-in-a-bucket behavior becomes normalized. Gatekeeping, sabotage, and zero-sum thinking dominate. Collaboration becomes impossible because everyone is guarding their small piece.

## Real-Life Symptoms {#real-life-symptoms}

- **Leaders hoarding information** instead of mentoring
- **Colleagues refusing to teach** others for fear of being replaced
- **Politicians looting** because "it's our turn to eat"
- **Business owners** underpaying staff because "I struggled too"
- **Successful people** pulling the ladder up behind them
- **Envy of others' success** instead of inspiration

## Why It Persists {#why-it-persists}

Centuries of actual scarcity—colonial extraction, resource competition, economic instability—have etched this pattern deep. Even when abundance exists, the **fear of lack** remains.

## How to Break Free {#how-to-break-free}

1. **Study abundance** — Read about economies of scale, network effects, and how wealth actually multiplies when shared.

2. **Teach what you know** — Giving knowledge away proves it doesn't deplete you.

3. **Collaborate before competing** — Ask "how can we grow the pie?" before "how do I get my slice?"

4. **Celebrate others' wins** — Train yourself to feel joy, not threat, when others succeed.

5. **Create systems of mutual benefit** — Structure arrangements where everyone wins.

6. **Invest in others** — Putting resources into people is the ultimate abundance move.

## The Shift {#the-shift}

From: *"If they win, I lose"*

To: *"When we all win, I win bigger"*
