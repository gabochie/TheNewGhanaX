## Description {#description}

A pervasive mindset where **everything is about quick gain**. Get rich quick schemes, immediate gratification, and "sakawa planning"—making moves for today's cash with no thought for tomorrow's consequences.

## The Effect {#the-effect}

Lack of generational thinking. No investment in systems or structures. Infrastructure crumbles. Businesses never mature. The country stays stuck in cycles of boom and bust.

## Real-Life Symptoms {#real-life-symptoms}

- **Road contracts** that crumble within a year
- **Politicians thinking** only in 4-year election cycles
- **Businesses** focused on today's profit, not tomorrow's brand
- **Youth** falling for Ponzi schemes and fraud
- **Sakawa culture** — the glorification of quick, ill-gotten gains
- **Environmental destruction** for immediate extraction
- **Building without permits** because "we'll fix it later"

## Why It Persists {#why-it-persists}

When survival is urgent, long-term thinking feels like a luxury. Combine that with:
- **Weak institutions** that don't punish short-termism
- **Cultural celebration** of sudden wealth, regardless of source
- **Lack of consequences** for those who cut corners
- **Economic instability** that makes the future feel uncertain

## How to Break Free {#how-to-break-free}

1. **Ask "what happens in 10 years?"** — Make this a habit before every major decision.

2. **Study compound interest** — Financial and otherwise. Small, consistent efforts create massive results.

3. **Build systems, not just income** — Create things that work without you.

4. **Delay gratification** — Practice saying no to immediate rewards for larger future ones.

5. **Document your plans** — Writing forces longer-term thinking.

6. **Find long-term mentors** — Surround yourself with people playing the long game.

## The Shift {#the-shift}

From: *"Make money fast by any means"*

To: *"Build something that outlasts me"*
