Trap #5

Short-Termism

Everything is about quick gain—'make money fast' by any means

A pervasive mindset where everything is about quick gain. Get rich quick schemes, immediate gratification, and "sakawa planning"—making moves for today's cash with no thought for tomorrow's consequences.

What It Is

A pervasive mindset where **everything is about quick gain**. Get rich quick schemes, immediate gratification, and "sakawa planning"—making moves for today's cash with no thought for tomorrow's consequences.

The Effect

Infrastructure crumbles in a year. Businesses collapse after initial hype. Politicians plan only for the next election. No one builds for generations because everyone needs to eat now.

Real-Life Symptoms

  • Ponzi schemes promising impossible returns
  • Roads built poorly to save costs, then fail immediately
  • Businesses focused on launch hype, not long-term value
  • Political planning only in 4-year election cycles
  • Get rich quick obsession over steady wealth building

Why It Persists

Economic instability makes the future feel uncertain, weak institutions don't punish corner-cutting, and cultural celebration of sudden wealth regardless of source.

How to Break Free

  1. Ask "what happens in 10 years?" — Make it a habit before every decision.
  2. Study compound interest — Small consistent efforts create massive results.
  3. Build systems, not just income — Create things that work without you.
  4. Practice delayed gratification — Train yourself to wait for bigger rewards.
  5. Document your plans — Write down long-term goals and review regularly.

The Shift

From: "How can I make money today?"
To: "What can I build that lasts 10 years?"
