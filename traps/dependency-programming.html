Trap #8

Dependency Programming

Belief that salvation must come from 'above'

A deep-seated belief that salvation must come from "above"—government, IMF, NGOs, foreign donors, or some external rescuer. The mindset of waiting to be saved rather than rising to save yourself.

What It Is

A deep-seated belief that **salvation must come from "above"**—government, IMF, NGOs, foreign donors, or some external rescuer. The mindset of waiting to be saved rather than rising to save yourself.

The Effect

Citizens wait to be rescued instead of rising to solve problems. IMF loans are celebrated as victories. Local initiatives die waiting for external validation or funding.

Real-Life Symptoms

  • Celebrating IMF loans as national victories
  • Waiting for government to fix community problems
  • Foreign aid dependency instead of local solutions
  • "They should help us" mentality
  • NGO preference over local businesses

Why It Persists

Colonial economies were extractive not self-sustaining, post-independence nation-building struggled, aid industry incentives benefit from dependency, and learned helplessness from decades of being told help is needed.

How to Break Free

  1. Start with what you can control — Don't wait for perfect conditions.
  2. Build local solutions — Without waiting for external intervention.
  3. Question every handout — What's the true impact?
  4. Pay for local value — Even when aid is free.
  5. Vote for builders — Leaders who create systems, not distribute favors.

The Shift

From: "When will they help us?"
To: "How can we help ourselves?"
