Trap #6

Religious Over-Spiritualization

Praying for what requires planning

Using prayer and spiritual activity as a substitute for practical action and planning. Binding demons that require discipline. Fasting for what requires studying. "God will do it" as an excuse for inaction.

What It Is

Using prayer and spiritual activity as a substitute for **practical action and planning**. Binding demons that require discipline. Fasting for what requires studying. "God will do it" as an excuse for inaction.

The Effect

Passivity disguised as faith. Real problems go unaddressed because they're "spiritualized" instead of solved. Entire generations wait for divine intervention while refusing human effort.

Real-Life Symptoms

  • Fasting to pass exams without studying
  • Anointing businesses that lack strategy
  • Prayer camps for medical conditions that need hospitals
  • "God will provide" as a budget plan
  • Church attendance replacing skill development
  • Prophecies about wealth without work
  • Spiritual warfare against problems that need systems

Why It Persists

Prayer feels easier than hard work, religious gatherings provide belonging, "spiritual attacks" externalize personal failures, and some religious leaders benefit from dependent followers.

How to Break Free

  1. Pray, then move — Faith without works is dead. Let prayer fuel action, not replace it.
  2. Study your scriptures properly — Most sacred texts emphasize work, wisdom, and stewardship.
  3. Set measurable goals — "God will provide" isn't a KPI. Define what success looks like.
  4. Invest in skills — God works through trained hands.
  5. Discern spiritual vs. practical problems — Some issues need prayer. Others need planning.
  6. Find faith communities that build — Churches that teach skills, not just blessings.

The Shift

From: "I'm praying for a breakthrough"
To: "I'm working like it depends on me, praying like it depends on God"
